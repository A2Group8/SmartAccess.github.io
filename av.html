Autonomous Vehicles

Intro

Autonomous Vehicles, most commonly known as self-driving cars, have become a popular subject since it has sparked to public with the debut of Google’s Self driving cars ‘Waymo’, Uber’s self-driving car fleet and the most popular of them all, the major spark Tesla, the first company to give autonomous vehicles to consumers.

What does it do?

An Autonomous vehicle, as its name suggests, is a vehicle that has the ability to guide itself safely without any human interaction. Throughout the last 5 years autonomous vehicles have gone from “maybe possible” to “definitely possible” to “inevitable”. (Davies, 2018) From Ride hailing companies like Uber and Lyft to Car manufacturers like Ford and Volvo to Tech Giants like Google and IBM, everyone is trying their best to come up with a fool proof fully autonomous car. While none have been perfect to date, there are numerous cars that drive themselves prowling the streets of the United States at the time we speak, being tested thoroughly through trial and error. At the current stage of development, taking into consideration the most publicly available Self driving car, the Tesla Model S, even though they call it an autonomous car, we really see that it doesn’t have the capability in taking you from a point A to B without any human interactions just yet, hence the term Semi-autonomous is perhaps the most suitable for it, as it requires a human driver to intervene, at least for the time being. Even for such a car with such technology, several crashes have been documented with several cases of passenger deaths, instances of pedestrian deaths, its proven that we have still a long way to go be enjoying a fully autonomous car. While the full capabilities of a Self-Driving car are yet to be revealed, according to Tesla CEO Elon Musk we are just 2 years away from ‘sleeping in our cars’, whether that’s true is yet to be seen. As another angle of view, a self-driving car can also be programmed to reduce emission through controlled economical driving styles, creating a positive impact for the environment.

What is the likely impact?

This technology, if perfected can have a huge impact on the world, and life itself. People will no longer need to drive every day, in fact driving could be something of the past if and when Self-driving cars get widespread and the prices go low, as it will be an expensive and exclusive feature during the first few years before its widely adopted and spreads. With the widespread of autonomous cars, the traffic conditions will likely improve, considering vehicles won’t increase largely in numbers, and also will likely reduce traffic accidents and car crashes, increasing the overall safety of passengers and third party, which according to ‘The Economist’ could lead to a shortage of Organ donors (many of whom are young people killed in car accidents) due to lesser deaths. According to data crunchers at Intel, autonomous cars could impact the global economy by adding $7 Trillion dollars to the economy. (Marshall, 2017).Even Public Transportation, Taxis and ride hailing will be cheaper than ever, driverless Mobile shops will also be an advantage to be taken by retailer. As adverse effects, this revolutionized autonomous vehicle industry will lead to the loss of several jobs for Taxi Drivers as Autonomous cars takeover, Bus Drivers and Trains Drivers due to autonomous Buses and Trains taking over their jobs. As far as it goes in destroying jobs, it also does bring some new jobs with it, like Jobs in Monitoring Autonomous Systems, Fleet Response, Dispatchers and 3D-Mapping. While it won’t have the opportunity to create much entirely new jobs apart from the aforementioned, there will be more job openings for Technicians, Engineers and in Customer Service field, though still there will be no replacement jobs for those who lost them. So, concluding the autonomous cars will impact towards a safer, effective and efficient world.

How will this affect you?

Autonomous cars will revolutionize daily life in an unimaginable way, helping save time as millions could do their work, while travelling, minimizing wasted time, even more so due to lesser traffic. Another upside would be that a rider of an autonomous vehicle could text or even drink to their heart’s content without endangering anyone else (The Economist, 2018). People could even use the travelling period for productivity or pretty much anything else. We could get our next ride to the airport for much cheaper and faster, our next delivery will be at our doorstep for faster and cheaper, your next shoes might be bought from a mobile shop parked at your driveway or the next meeting you’ll go attend will be while travelling in an autonomous car. People could even use the travelling period for productivity or pretty much anything else, so everybody will have free time. According to the WHO, Self-Driving cars will help save 1.25 Million lives that are lost each year, and reduce 20-50 million that are injured every year. This will be of most benefit for people with disabilities, elder family member can be monitored even throughout their journey, with the help of the autonomous cars and they will also have the ability to travel alone hence, mobility for everyone will improve dramatically. This will change everyone’s life in the same way, giving more time, easing lifestyle and improvements in mobility. This wouldn’t have a different effect on every person as it will be the normal method of transport, it will be affecting everyone the same, except the businesses having a different impact, overall changing everyone’s daily life.
