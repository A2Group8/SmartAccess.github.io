Computer Scientist

Computer Science is mainly the study of computers. Computer Scientists deal mostly with software and software systems (theory, development, design, development and application)

Government agencies and private software publishers

Computer Scientist often work as part of a research team with computer programmers, I.T professionals and electrical or mechanical engineers.

Encryption and quantum computing.

Software Developer

Run, design and test various computer programs and applications

Software Developers spend most of their time within the company or firm. Some work as a freelancer.

Having unspecific requirements, poor documentation, inefficient development processes and outdated technology.

Web Developer

Web Developers design, create and modify websites. They are mainly responsible for maintaining a user friendly, stable website that offers the necessary functionality for their clients needs.

Web Developers work with clients and investors.

Web Developers usually work for a company or firm. Some Web Developers offer their services on a freelance basis.

Internet Explorer is by far one of the biggest challenges that Web Developers face. Even if the website looks perfect in every other browser, Internet Explorer always has quirks which need to be addressed. Other challenges also include task automation and performance.

I.T Support Professional

Monitor and maintain the computer systems and networks of an organisation ensuring that the day-to-day operations work.

I.T Support Professionals work with external clients.

I.T Support Professional mainly work within an organisation (eg. office, schools, etc).

Business Analyst

Conducting market analysis, analysing product lines and overall profitability of the business.

The business analyst discuss information technology with tech people, business managers, and end users in order to find improvements for business operations and processes.

Business Analyst mainly work for a firm or company.
